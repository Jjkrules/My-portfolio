About  Me!
The Journey
As I got into high school, my curiosity grew into a deeper interest in how software works. Once I started programming, everything clicked I enjoyed breaking down problems and building solutions that felt like real-world puzzle solving.

I went on to study Computer Science at Temple University, where I built a strong foundation in software development, data structures, and algorithms. I also worked as a Teaching Assistant, helping students strengthen their problem-solving skills while deepening my own.

Today, I apply that same mindset at Green Expert Technology, developing and optimizing mission-critical software for U.S. Navy Systems with a focus on reliability and performance.

The Spark
My interest in technology started early. My parents got our first computer when I was about three years old, and from then on, I was always on it exploring, experimenting, and figuring things out on my own.

At first, it was just curiosity. I would click through everything, try new things, and slowly start to understand how systems worked. As I got older, that curiosity turned into something more. I became the person people went to when something broke whether it was fixing software issues or troubleshooting devices.

That early exposure made me realize that technology wasn’t just something I used it was something I could understand, control, and eventually build

The Future
I aim to grow as a software engineer by building scaleable, high-performance systems where reliability truly matters. I’m focused on strengthening my skills in backend development, system design, and modern technologies like cloud and containerization.

Long-term, I want to take on greater responsibility in designing and leading impactful projects while continuing to learn, improve, and help others along the way.
